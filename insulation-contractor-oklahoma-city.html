Why Oklahoma City Homes Need Proper Insulation

Oklahoma City sits right in the heart of the Southern Great Plains, which means extreme weather is a fact of life. Summers regularly bring triple-digit heat, with the urban heat island effect making central OKC neighborhoods even hotter. Winters can plunge into the single digits, and the infamous ice storms — like the devastating events of 2007 and 2020 — push heating systems to their limits. Between those extremes, spring brings high winds and severe thunderstorms that test the integrity of every home's building envelope.

Without proper insulation, Oklahoma City homeowners are essentially throwing money out the window. The Department of Energy estimates that 25-30% of residential heating and cooling energy is wasted due to inadequate insulation. In a city where summers last five months and winters can be bitterly cold, that adds up to hundreds of dollars every year. Proper insulation from Bo's Insulation can reduce your heating and cooling costs by 25-40%, making your Oklahoma City home dramatically more comfortable while lowering your bills.

Neighborhoods We Serve Across Oklahoma City

We work in every corner of Oklahoma City. Each neighborhood has its own character and its own insulation needs:

  • Mesta Park & Heritage Hills — These historic districts feature some of OKC's oldest homes, many built between 1900 and 1930. Original insulation is often minimal or completely degraded. We specialize in injection foam to insulate these walls without disturbing the historic character.
  • Paseo Arts District & Plaza District — Charming bungalows and cottages from the 1920s-1940s that frequently have uninsulated wall cavities and thin attic insulation. Blown-in attic insulation and air sealing make a dramatic difference here.
  • Nichols Hills & The Village — Mid-century and newer homes that often have settling or compressed insulation. Attic top-ups and wall injection foam restore their energy performance.
  • Capitol Hill & South OKC — A mix of older craftsman homes and post-war construction. Many of these homes have never been re-insulated and benefit enormously from a comprehensive insulation upgrade.
  • NW Expressway Corridor & Putnam City Area — Ranch-style homes from the 1960s-1980s that typically have fiberglass batts that have lost their effectiveness over the decades.
  • Edgemere Park, Crown Heights & Gatewood — Urban core neighborhoods with a rich mix of architectural styles, all benefiting from modern insulation solutions.
