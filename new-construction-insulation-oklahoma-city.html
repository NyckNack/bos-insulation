Insulation Options for New Homes in Oklahoma City

New construction gives you the widest range of insulation options because all wall cavities, attic spaces, and rim joists are fully accessible before drywall is installed. Here are the primary insulation types we install in new OKC homes:

Spray Foam Insulation

Spray foam is the premium option for new construction and delivers the highest performance of any insulation material. Open-cell spray foam applied to the underside of the roof deck creates a conditioned attic space — keeping your HVAC ducts within the thermal envelope and eliminating the brutal 150-degree attic temperatures that OKC summers produce. Closed-cell spray foam is ideal for rim joists, crawl spaces, and areas requiring a vapor barrier. Many OKC builders are choosing spray foam as their standard insulation because it dramatically reduces callback complaints about comfort and energy bills.

Blown-In Fiberglass

Blown-in fiberglass insulation is a cost-effective option for new construction walls and attics. Using a dense-pack technique in wall cavities, blown-in fiberglass fills around wiring, plumbing, and electrical boxes more completely than traditional batts. In attics, it can be blown to any depth to achieve the target R-value. For OKC builders who need solid performance at a competitive price point, blown-in fiberglass is an excellent choice.

Batt Insulation

Fiberglass batts remain the most commonly specified insulation for new construction walls in the Oklahoma City market. When installed correctly — with no gaps, compression, or voids — batts provide reliable thermal performance at the lowest cost per square foot. The key is quality installation, and that is where many insulation crews fall short. Bo's Insulation takes the time to cut batts precisely around outlets, pipes, and wiring, ensuring full-cavity fill without compression. Our batt installations consistently pass inspection on the first visit.

Hybrid Systems

Many of the most energy-efficient new homes in Oklahoma City use a hybrid insulation approach. A common combination is spray foam on the roof deck and rim joists with blown-in fiberglass or batts in the walls. This delivers the air-sealing benefits of spray foam where they matter most while keeping overall project costs reasonable. We help builders design the right insulation package for each home's specs and price point.
