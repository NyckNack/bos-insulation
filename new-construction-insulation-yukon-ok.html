Insulation Options for New Homes in Yukon

New construction in Yukon provides full access to wall cavities and attic spaces before drywall, making it the ideal time to install the best insulation for your home. Here are the options we offer Yukon builders and homeowners:

Spray Foam — Wind-Resistant Air Sealing

Yukon's western plains exposure makes air infiltration a significant concern. Wind-driven air leakage through gaps in the building envelope can make a home drafty, uncomfortable, and expensive to heat and cool — even if the insulation itself has adequate R-value. Spray foam solves this problem because it expands to fill every crack, seam, and void, creating a continuous air barrier that wind cannot penetrate. Open-cell spray foam in the attic ($1.50-$2.50/sq ft) creates a sealed, conditioned space that eliminates the most common source of energy loss in Yukon homes. Closed-cell spray foam in crawl spaces and rim joists ($2.50-$4.00/sq ft) adds moisture resistance and structural strength.

Blown-In Fiberglass — Complete Cavity Fill

Dense-pack blown-in fiberglass is an excellent wall insulation option for Yukon new builds. The material fills completely around electrical wires, plumbing pipes, and outlet boxes, eliminating the gaps that are common with hand-cut batts. In attics, blown-in fiberglass can be installed to any depth to achieve the required R-value. For Yukon builders who need solid thermal performance at a price point that keeps new homes competitive in the market, blown-in fiberglass delivers reliable results.

Fiberglass Batts — Budget-Friendly Performance

Standard fiberglass batts are still the most commonly specified wall insulation in Yukon's new construction market. The material is affordable and effective when installed correctly. Bo's Insulation installs batts to Grade I standards — meaning full cavity fill with precise cuts around every obstacle, no compression, and no voids. Properly installed batts provide consistent thermal performance that meets code requirements at the lowest per-square-foot cost.

Hybrid Systems for Maximum Value

Many Yukon builders and homebuyers find the best value in a hybrid approach. A common package combines spray foam on the roof deck and rim joists with blown-in fiberglass or batts in the exterior walls. This captures the critical air-sealing benefit of spray foam where it matters most — the attic and foundation perimeter — while keeping overall costs manageable. For Yukon homes exposed to persistent winds, this hybrid approach dramatically outperforms an all-batt installation.
