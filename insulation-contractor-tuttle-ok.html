Tuttle's Trusted Insulation Contractor for New Builds & Existing Homes

Tuttle has undergone a remarkable transformation. A generation ago, this Grady County community southwest of Oklahoma City was a quiet rural town where cattle outnumbered people and everyone knew their neighbors by name. Today, Tuttle is one of the fastest-growing cities in the entire OKC metro, with a population that has nearly tripled since the early 2000s. Families are drawn here by the award-winning Tuttle Public Schools, the generous lot sizes, and the chance to own a newer home without the density and traffic of the inner suburbs.

That growth has created a unique insulation landscape. Unlike older OKC metro communities where most of our work involves upgrading aging insulation, Tuttle's housing stock is overwhelmingly new. The majority of homes here were built in the 2010s and 2020s, and new subdivisions continue to rise along Highway 37 and Tuttle Road. Bo's Insulation works with both homebuilders during the construction phase and homeowners who want to upgrade or correct insulation in their existing Tuttle properties. The Bohannan brothers personally inspect every job site in Tuttle — we understand that in a close-knit community like this, our reputation is everything.

Insulation for Tuttle's Distinct Housing Types

Tuttle is not a cookie-cutter suburb. The community includes everything from large-scale development homes to custom builds on five-acre parcels to century-old farmsteads. Each property type demands a different insulation approach:

  • New Construction & Development Homes — The subdivisions sprouting along Tuttle Road, near the schools, and off Highway 37 represent the bulk of Tuttle's housing. These homes are built to modern code, but code-minimum insulation is not the same as optimal insulation. Builders who partner with Bo's Insulation can offer their buyers spray foam upgrades that deliver noticeably better comfort and energy performance. For homeowners who purchased a production-built home and find certain rooms uncomfortably hot or cold, we can identify where the original insulation falls short and make targeted improvements.
  • Custom Homes on Acreage — Many Tuttle residents chose this community specifically because they could build a custom home on a spacious lot. These larger homes with tall ceilings, bonus rooms above garages, and expansive attic spaces need insulation that is carefully planned during design and expertly installed during construction. Spray foam in the roofline eliminates the traditional vented-attic approach and turns the entire building envelope into a conditioned space — a smart choice for Tuttle's wide-open, wind-exposed terrain.
  • Older Farmhouses & Rural Properties — Scattered among the new development, you will still find original Tuttle-area homes — ranch houses from the 1960s and 1970s, farmsteads built mid-century, and the occasional pre-war structure on agricultural land. These properties almost universally have inadequate insulation. Wall cavities are often empty, attic insulation has settled to just a few inches, and air leaks are extensive. Injection foam for the walls combined with a full attic insulation upgrade can cut heating and cooling costs by 30-50% in these older homes.
  • Properties Near Bridge Creek — The area south and west of Tuttle, stretching toward Bridge Creek, includes rural homes and acreage properties that face extreme wind exposure. This corridor was devastated by the May 3, 1999 tornado, and many homes in the area were rebuilt in the early 2000s. Proper insulation and air sealing are critical here — not just for energy efficiency, but to prevent wind-driven rain infiltration and to maintain structural integrity of the building envelope during severe weather.

Why Insulation Matters in Tuttle's Exposed Terrain

Tuttle sits on open prairie southwest of OKC with little natural windbreak. Proper insulation is your home's first line of defense against the elements.

☀

Relentless Summer Heat

With no tall buildings or dense tree canopy to provide shade, Tuttle homes absorb direct sun from every angle. Attic temperatures can exceed 150 degrees, and without adequate insulation, that heat radiates down into your living space all day and night.

❄

Unobstructed Winter Winds

Cold fronts barrel across Grady County's flat terrain with nothing to slow them down. Homes on larger lots and acreage properties are especially vulnerable. Insulated and air-sealed walls prevent wind-driven heat loss that can double your heating bills.

🌪

Severe Weather & Tornado Risk

Tuttle and nearby Bridge Creek sit in one of Oklahoma's most active severe weather corridors. The May 3, 1999 tornado carved a path through this area. A properly sealed and insulated building envelope resists wind-driven moisture and helps protect your home's structure during storms.

💰

Energy Savings & Rebates

Tuttle homeowners can reduce heating and cooling costs by 25-40% with proper insulation. Most properties here are served by OG&E, which offers rebates on qualifying insulation and air sealing upgrades. Some Grady County properties may qualify for PSO rebates instead.
