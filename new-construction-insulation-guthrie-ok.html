Insulation Options for New Homes in Guthrie

New construction in Guthrie gives builders and homeowners the full range of insulation choices. Here is what we install in Guthrie's new homes:

Spray Foam — Ideal for Open-Terrain Homes

Many new homes in the Guthrie area are built on acreage with significant wind exposure. These properties face unobstructed north winds in winter and constant air pressure against exterior walls that can drive infiltration through any gap in the building envelope. Spray foam insulation is uniquely suited for these conditions because it creates a continuous, monolithic air barrier that eliminates the pathways wind uses to enter a home. Open-cell spray foam on the roof deck ($1.50-$2.50/sq ft) seals the attic completely, while closed-cell foam on rim joists and crawl spaces ($2.50-$4.00/sq ft) provides moisture resistance along with its thermal and air-sealing benefits.

Blown-In Fiberglass — Reliable Cavity Fill

Dense-pack blown-in fiberglass is a strong option for wall cavities in Guthrie new construction. The material flows around wiring, plumbing, and other obstructions to achieve complete cavity fill — something that is difficult to accomplish with hand-cut batts, especially in homes with complex wall framing or non-standard stud spacing. In attics, blown-in fiberglass can be installed to the higher depths required in Climate Zone 4, meeting the R-49 attic requirement cost-effectively.

Fiberglass Batts — Affordable Foundation

Fiberglass batts provide reliable thermal performance at the lowest per-square-foot cost. For Guthrie builders and homeowners working within a defined budget, properly installed batts meet code requirements and deliver consistent performance. Bo's Insulation installs every batt to Grade I standards — no gaps, no compression, no voids — ensuring that the R-value printed on the label is the R-value you actually get in your walls.

Acreage Home Considerations

Homes built on acreage near Guthrie often have features that require specialized insulation attention. Pier-and-beam foundations with ventilated crawl spaces need closed-cell spray foam to prevent moisture issues and provide thermal protection from below. Metal buildings or barndominiums — increasingly popular on Guthrie-area acreage — require spray foam applied directly to the metal skin to prevent condensation and provide thermal control. Detached garages, workshops, and outbuildings connected to the home's HVAC system also need proper insulation to avoid wasting energy. Bo's Insulation handles all of these applications with the same quality and attention to detail we bring to every project.
