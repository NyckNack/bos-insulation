Warr Acres' Trusted Insulation Contractor

Warr Acres is one of Oklahoma City's most distinctive pocket cities — a tight-knit community of roughly 10,000 residents completely surrounded by OKC. Named after Major Gruver P. Warr, a World War II veteran, Warr Acres has maintained its own identity since its incorporation, with tree-lined streets, well-kept yards, and a neighborhood feel that larger suburbs cannot replicate.

The housing stock tells a clear story. Nearly every home in Warr Acres was built during the 1950s and 1960s, creating remarkably uniform neighborhoods of mid-century brick ranch homes. These homes were well-built for their era, but insulation standards were minimal at the time of construction. After 60 to 70 years, most original insulation has settled, degraded, or simply was never sufficient to begin with. Bo's Insulation specializes in bringing these classic Warr Acres homes up to modern energy-efficiency standards without altering their character.

Insulation Challenges in Warr Acres' Mid-Century Homes

Because Warr Acres' housing stock is so consistent, we see the same insulation problems again and again across the community:

  • Thin or Settled Attic Insulation — Most 1950s-1960s Warr Acres homes were built with just 2-4 inches of attic insulation, far below the R-38 to R-49 recommended for Oklahoma today. Over six decades, that insulation has settled and compressed, leaving your home exposed to extreme summer heat radiating through the ceiling.
  • Uninsulated or Poorly Insulated Walls — Many Warr Acres brick ranch homes have hollow wall cavities with little to no insulation. Injection foam fills these cavities without any demolition, making a dramatic difference in both comfort and energy bills.
  • Significant Air Leakage — Decades of settling, shifting, and wear have opened gaps around plumbing penetrations, electrical boxes, window frames, and attic bypasses. Air sealing these pathways is often the single most impactful upgrade for a Warr Acres home.
  • Outdated or Damaged Materials — Some Warr Acres homes still contain original fiberglass batts or even vermiculite insulation that has degraded well past its useful life. Removal and replacement with modern blown-in or spray foam insulation restores proper thermal performance.
