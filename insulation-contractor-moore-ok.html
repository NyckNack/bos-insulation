Moore's Resilient Homes Deserve Expert Insulation

Moore, Oklahoma has earned a reputation as one of the most resilient communities in the country. The devastating tornadoes of 1999, 2003, 2010, and the catastrophic May 2013 EF5 tornado tested this city in ways few communities ever experience. Through it all, Moore has rebuilt — not just to the same standards, but stronger and better. That rebuilding effort created a unique housing landscape: modern, code-compliant homes standing alongside older residences that survived the storms, creating a patchwork of construction eras and insulation needs that few other cities can match.

Bo's Insulation has worked extensively in Moore, and we understand the specific challenges homeowners face here. Whether your Moore home was rebuilt after a tornado or has been standing since the 1970s, our team knows exactly what your home needs to perform at its best.

The Unique Insulation Landscape in Moore

Moore's housing stock tells the story of the city's cycles of destruction and renewal. Each chapter brings different insulation characteristics:

  • Post-Tornado Rebuilds (2013-Present) — Thousands of Moore homes were rebuilt after the 2013 tornado, constructed to updated building codes. While these homes are relatively new, many homeowners report issues like rooms that will not stay cool, uneven temperatures between floors, and utility bills higher than expected. Common culprits include insufficient attic insulation depth, missed air sealing around recessed lights and ductwork penetrations, and gaps behind bathtubs on exterior walls. Bo's Insulation addresses these overlooked areas to bring these newer Moore homes up to their full potential.
  • 1980s-2000s Subdivisions — Moore experienced massive suburban growth during these decades. Homes from this era — found in neighborhoods along SW 4th Street, near the Warren Theatre area, and east of I-35 — were built with basic fiberglass batts that have deteriorated significantly over two to four decades. Attic insulation has settled below effective R-values, and wall cavities were often minimally insulated. These Moore homes see the most dramatic improvement from our comprehensive insulation upgrades.
  • Original Moore Homes (1960s-1970s) — The oldest homes in Moore, concentrated near downtown and along Eastern Avenue, represent the city's original suburban development. Many of these homes still have their original insulation — or barely any at all. Injection foam walls and new blown-in attic insulation can reduce energy costs by 30-40% in these older Moore residences.

Storm Recovery and Insulation

Moore homeowners understand storms better than most. What many do not realize is how significantly storm damage affects insulation performance, even when the damage seems minor. Roof leaks from hail or wind damage can saturate attic insulation, reducing its R-value by as much as 50% and creating a breeding ground for mold. Wind-driven rain can infiltrate wall cavities, compromising injection foam or fiberglass batts. Even moderate storms can shift blown-in insulation in the attic, creating bare spots over living spaces that become hot spots in summer and cold spots in winter.

After every storm season, Bo's Insulation recommends a thorough attic inspection for Moore homeowners. We will honestly assess whether your insulation was affected and only recommend work that is genuinely necessary. If your insulation is fine, we will tell you — even though it means we do not get the job. That is just how the Bohannan brothers operate.
