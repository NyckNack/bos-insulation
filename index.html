Frequently Asked Questions

How much does insulation cost in Oklahoma City?

It depends on the type and scope of the project. Attic blown-in insulation typically runs $1.00–$2.50 per square foot, spray foam $1.50–$4.00, and injection foam $2.00–$3.50. We offer free on-site estimates so you know the exact cost upfront — no surprises.

What type of insulation is best for Oklahoma homes?

Oklahoma's wild temperature swings — brutal summers and freezing winters — require insulation that handles both extremes. For most homes, we recommend blown-in cellulose or fiberglass in the attic combined with injection foam in exterior walls. Spray foam is ideal for crawl spaces and areas that need air sealing too.

Do you offer free estimates?

Absolutely. We'll come to your home, assess the situation, and give you an honest quote. If the job doesn't make sense for your home, we'll tell you — even if it means we don't get the work. That's just how we operate.

What areas do you serve?

We serve the entire OKC metro: Oklahoma City, Edmond, Norman, Moore, Yukon, Mustang, Midwest City, Del City, Bethany, Warr Acres, The Village, Nichols Hills, and surrounding communities.

Will new insulation really lower my energy bills?

Yes. Proper insulation typically reduces heating and cooling costs by 25–40%, depending on your home's current condition. Many of our customers see the difference on their very first utility bill. Plus, you may qualify for OGE rebates that offset the upfront cost.

How long does insulation installation take?

Most residential insulation projects are completed in a single day. Larger homes or multi-area projects may take 1–2 days. We'll give you an accurate timeline with your free estimate.
