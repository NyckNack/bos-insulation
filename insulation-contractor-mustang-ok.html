Mustang's Fast-Growing Community Needs Quality Insulation

Mustang, Oklahoma has experienced remarkable transformation over the past two decades. What was once a quiet agricultural community southwest of Oklahoma City has become one of the metro's most popular suburbs, known for its award-winning Mustang Public Schools, family-friendly atmosphere, and steady stream of new housing developments. The population has grown from under 14,000 in 2000 to well over 23,000 today, and that growth shows no signs of slowing down.

That rapid expansion has created a distinctive housing landscape in Mustang. The city center retains its original small-town character, with homes dating back to the 1950s through the 1970s, while the surrounding areas feature wave after wave of subdivision development from the 1990s to present day. Each generation of construction brought different insulation standards, materials, and methods — and each now presents its own set of challenges for homeowners looking to maximize comfort and minimize energy costs.

Bo's Insulation has been serving Mustang homeowners for over eight years. The Bohannan brothers, Keyton and Nicholas, understand the insulation needs of this growing community because they have worked in homes spanning every era of Mustang's development. We specialize exclusively in existing homes — not new construction — which means we bring a focused expertise to diagnosing and solving the insulation problems that Mustang homeowners face.

Insulation Challenges Across Mustang's Neighborhoods

  • Original Mustang & Downtown Area — The oldest part of Mustang, centered around Highway 152 (State Highway 37) and the Mustang Road corridor, features ranch homes and modest cottages from the 1950s through the 1970s. These homes were built when insulation was almost an afterthought — many have thin fiberglass batts in the attic, no wall insulation at all, and significant air leakage around windows, doors, and utility penetrations. For these original Mustang homes, a comprehensive upgrade including injection foam walls, blown-in attic insulation, and air sealing can reduce energy bills by 35-40%.
  • 1990s-2000s Subdivisions — The first major wave of suburban development in Mustang produced neighborhoods south of Highway 152 and along Sara Road. These homes were built to the insulation codes of their era, which are far below today's standards. After 20-30 years, the original fiberglass batts have settled and lost significant R-value. Homeowners in these Mustang neighborhoods consistently tell us about hot upstairs bedrooms in summer and drafty rooms in winter — problems that proper attic insulation and air sealing resolve.
  • Newer Developments & Southwest Mustang — The latest growth in Mustang has pushed south and west, with new subdivisions featuring larger homes built to current code. Even these newer homes benefit from insulation attention — builder-grade installations often hit minimum code requirements without achieving optimal performance. Common issues include insufficient attic insulation depth, missed air sealing around recessed lights and plumbing penetrations, and gaps in bonus room and knee wall insulation.
