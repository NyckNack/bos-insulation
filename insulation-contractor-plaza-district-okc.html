Insulation Challenges in Plaza District Homes

The housing stock around the Plaza District presents a consistent set of insulation problems tied to the era of construction:

  • 1920s-1930s Craftsman Bungalows — These are the architectural gems of the Plaza District. Built with pride and craftsmanship, they also predate modern insulation standards entirely. Most have balloon-frame walls with absolutely nothing in the wall cavities, attics with a thin layer of old mineral wool or no insulation at all, and significant air leakage throughout. Injection foam for the walls and blown-in insulation for the attic can transform the comfort and energy efficiency of these bungalows.
  • 1940s-1950s Small Frame Homes — The post-war homes in the Plaza area tend to be compact, efficient floor plans with frame construction. While some had basic fiberglass batts installed, decades of settling and compression have left most of that original insulation ineffective. Topping up attic insulation and adding wall injection foam makes a major difference in these homes.
  • Renovated Homes with Overlooked Insulation — The Plaza District has seen a wave of renovations as new owners update kitchens, bathrooms, and living spaces. Unfortunately, insulation is frequently the last thing considered during a renovation. We regularly work with homeowners who spent thousands on beautiful updates only to realize their walls are still empty and their attic is bare. The good news is that insulation can be added after a renovation without disturbing finished surfaces.
  • Duplexes and Multi-Unit Conversions — Several Plaza District properties started as duplexes or have been divided and recombined over the years. These homes often have unusual wall configurations and shared walls that create insulation gaps. A professional assessment identifies exactly where insulation is needed.

Why Insulation Matters for Plaza District Homes

Oklahoma's temperature swings are especially hard on the older bungalows and frame homes in the Plaza District. Proper insulation is the single most effective upgrade for comfort and energy savings.

☀

Sweltering Summers

Plaza District bungalows with uninsulated attics turn into ovens during Oklahoma's 100-degree summers. Your AC runs nonstop trying to overcome the heat radiating through the ceiling. Proper attic insulation makes a dramatic, immediate difference.

❄

Cold, Drafty Winters

Empty wall cavities and minimal attic insulation in older Plaza District homes let cold air infiltrate freely. Rooms feel cold no matter how high you set the heat. Injection foam and attic insulation eliminate these drafts for good.

🌪

Storm Season Impacts

Oklahoma severe weather can shift roofing materials and compromise attic seals on older homes. Post-storm insulation inspections catch problems before they quietly drive up your energy bills for months.

💰

OGE Rebates & Savings

Plaza District homeowners typically save 25-40% on heating and cooling after insulation upgrades. OGE utility rebates for qualifying improvements help offset the upfront cost, making insulation one of the smartest investments for older homes.
